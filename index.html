Srdjan Marinovic

About

I am a computer scientist with a research background and applied enginering experience in startups. My areas of interest include distributed streaming systems, information retrieval, runtime verification, knowledge bases, formal security models, non-monotonic AI, and applications of ML to graph analytics.

My software architectures are inspired by Parnas' information-hiding principles and layered virtual machines. My teams and I build iterative models and deploy fast: building complex systems is a process of exploration and discovery.

I serve as an ML advisor to Futurae. I provide practical and hands-on assistance in building authentication methods that adapt to users' usage patterns.

I am the technical co-founder and CTO of SignalFrame (acquired by PwC in 2021). We developed a temporal graph platform to ingest ~100 million vertices with ~1 billion edges per day. The platform built their embeddings in a streaming mode for temporal and spatial similarity analysis and predictive analytics. My technical contributions (both in terms of code and design) are:

  • (Vertex) Embeddings for temporal graphs,
  • Streaming framework with feedback loops (Golang),
  • Distributed graph query system (Golang),
  • Multi-agent classification system (Py and Scala),
  • Knowledge-base with Markov(-ish) semantics,
  • Monitoring and automated recovery of Kubernetes services.

Prior to SignalFrame, I was a senior researcher at ETH Zurich's Security Institute:

  • Database Security and Privacy – preventing probabilistic attacks on SQL DBs. Our algorithm used a Bayesian network synthesized from a probabilistic logic model and attack priors.
  • Distributed Systems Monitoring (with Google) – Developed metric temporal logics and streaming algorithms that handle missing and compromised system logs.
  • Decentralized Authorization Systems (with Kaba Security) – Developed tools to formally verify security models and their compositions in decentralized settings.

I received a PhD in Computing from Imperial College London where I developed a soft symbolic AI model, based on multi-valued logics, to ensemble decisions from unreliable and mutually-conflicting data sources. We applied it to securing pervasive medical workflows, as part of the EU-FP7 programme.
