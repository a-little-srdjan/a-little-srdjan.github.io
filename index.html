Srdjan Marinovic

About

I am a technical co-founder of a DC-based startup SignalFrame, where I lead the design and implementation of our spatio-temporal graph platform and algorithms. My areas of interest include distributed streaming systems, information retrieval, runtime monitoring, trust management, anomaly detection, non-monotonic AI, and application of machine learning to graph analytics. While at SignalFrame, my technical focus has been:

  • Data structures for temporal graphs
  • Graph data storage design
  • Streaming framework with feedback loops
  • Decentralized and distributed graph query system
  • Temporal graph embedding algorithm and analytics
  • Knowledge based founded on multi-agent continuous learning
  • Automated infrastructure monitoring and recovery

I have set up our software development guidelines and processes founded in Parnas' layered-codebase principles. Finally, I have contributed to VC pitches, product development and sales, and played an active part in establishing relationships with our customers.

Prior to SignalFrame, I worked as a senior researcher at ETH Zurich's Security Institute. I worked on

  • Database Security and Privacy – designed mechanisms to prevent probabilistic attacks on SQL DBs. We relied on a mixture of Bayesian models and logic models to develop an enforcement algorithm.
  • Distributed Systems Monitoring (with Google) – developed metric temporal logics to build streaming algorithms that operate under missing and compromised streaming events (logs).
  • Decentralized Authorization Systems (with Kaba Security) – designed trust management algorithms (ala Microsoft’s SPKI and OAuth2) for policy compositions and infrastructure failures. We also built policy verification tools based on automated theorem provers.

I received a PhD in Computing from Imperial College London where I developed a formal symbolic model, based on multi-valued logics, to ensemble decisions from unreliable and mutually-conflicting data sources. We applied it to build secure pervasive medical workflows, as part of the EU-FP7 programme.
