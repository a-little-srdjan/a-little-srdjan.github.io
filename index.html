Srdjan Marinovic

About

I am a computer scientist with enginering experience in startups. My areas of interest are distributed streaming systems, information retrieval, runtime verification, knowledge bases, formal security models, non-monotonic AI, and applications of ML to graph analytics.

My software architectures are inspired by Parnas' information-hiding principles and layered virtual machines. My teams build iterative models and deploy fast: building complex systems is a process of exploration and discovery.

I serve as an ML advisor to Futurae, where I am invloved with fraud-detection and adaptive-authentication models.

I was the technical co-founder and the CTO of SignalFrame (acquired by PwC in 2021). My team of 20 engineers (UI, mobile, backend, ML) and I built a streaming temporal graph platform. Our system collected data from 1 billion IoT devices per month, and ingested ~100 million vertices with ~1 billion edges per day. The platform built graph embeddings in a streaming mode for temporal and spatial predictive analytics.

My technical work was mostly focused on:

  • Temporal graph embeddings
  • Streaming framework with feedback loops (Golang)
  • Distributed graph query system (Golang)
  • Graph-pattern classifiers (Py and Scala)
  • Knowledge-base with Markov(-ish) semantics
  • Monitoring and automated recovery of Kubernetes services

In addition to my technical work, I regularly presented our products to our board and customers.

Prior to SignalFrame, I was a senior researcher at ETH Zurich's Security Institute:

  • Database Security and Privacy – preventing probabilistic attacks on SQL DBs. Our algorithm used a Bayesian network synthesized from a probabilistic logic model and attack priors.
  • Distributed Systems Monitoring (with Google) – metric temporal logics and streaming algorithms that handle missing and compromised system logs.
  • Decentralized Authorization Systems (with Kaba Security) – tools to formally verify security models and their compositions in decentralized settings.

I received a PhD in Computing from Imperial College London where I developed a soft symbolic AI model. Based on multi-valued logics, it ensembled decisions from unreliable and mutually-conflicting data sources. This model allowed us to build adaptive security models with continous monitoring and policy evaluation.
